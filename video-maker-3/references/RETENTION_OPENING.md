# Retention Opening — required script template

Every script the video-maker renders must open with this structure. It front-loads what
the video covers and gives a concrete reason to watch to the end (lifts watch-time and
retention). The mechanics are **persona-independent**; only the wording flexes to the host's
voice.

## The structure (insert at the very top of every script, before the body)

Beats in order:

1. **Signature open (optional).** If the persona has a fixed cold-open — channel name, host
   name, catchphrase, a recurring framing device — keep it first, 2–4 sentences. Skip if the
   character has none.
2. **Hook.** One true, vivid scene or one bold claim in the first 1–2 sentences, carrying
   emotional stakes or curiosity. End the hook with a single clear *promise* of what the
   viewer will walk away with.
3. **Preview ("here's what's coming").** Explicitly lay out, in order, the 3–4 things the
   video will cover. Use plain noun phrases that map 1:1 to the body chapters.
4. **Open loop ("reason to stay to the end").** Name that the most valuable item is saved
   for last — **without revealing what it is.** Tie it to a payoff the viewer wants (e.g.
   "the one that changed everything," "the mistake almost everyone makes," "the part nobody
   tells you").
5. **Transition** into the body.

…then the **body chapters**, then:

6. **Payoff (close the loop).** Near the end, explicitly deliver the saved-for-last item
   teased in beat 4, and call back to the promise from the hook so the loop visibly closes.
7. **Outro / sign-off** (whatever the channel normally uses).

## Generic phrasing templates (placeholders in CAPS)

- **Hook:** "[VIVID FIRST-PERSON SCENE or BOLD CLAIM]. [STAKES SENTENCE]. Here's [PROMISE]."
- **Preview:** "In the next few minutes I'm going to [CHAPTER 1], [CHAPTER 2], and [CHAPTER 3]."
- **Open loop:** "But stay with me to the very end, because [SAVED-FOR-LAST ITEM — described
  by its *payoff*, not its content] is [WHY IT MATTERS MOST]."
- **Payoff callback:** "Now, the thing I promised you at the start — [SAVED ITEM] — here it
  is. [DELIVER IT]."

## Tone adaptation (this is the key to making it work across characters)

Keep the **mechanics identical** for every persona (hook → preview → open loop → payoff →
callback). Flex only the **delivery** to the character's voice:

- **High-energy / hype host:** punchy, numbers, big claims, fast cuts of promises.
- **Warm / gentle host:** soften into an invitation — "let me lay out what we'll talk
  about… and do stay, because the last thing is the one I wish someone had told me."
- **Authoritative / expert host:** frame it as an agenda — "we'll cover X, Y, Z, and I've
  put the most important point last."

Rule of thumb: the open loop should sound like *the character*, never like a generic
YouTuber, while still doing the retention job. Never force hype phrasing onto a calm or
serious persona.

## Do / Don't

**Do**
- Make the preview map 1:1 to the real chapters.
- Keep the open loop **honest** — there must genuinely be a strong final payoff, and you
  must deliver it.
- Pay the loop off explicitly near the end (the callback is what makes it satisfying).
- Keep the whole preview + open loop tight: ~3–6 sentences.

**Don't**
- Don't reveal/spoil the saved-for-last item in the preview.
- Don't promise a payoff you don't deliver — it erodes trust fast.
- Don't list more than ~4 items, or it dilutes.
- Don't break character to sound salesy.

## Worked examples (showing it's persona-independent)

**Example A — energetic DIY/skills channel**

> "I glued two pieces of scrap wood together and it held up a 200-pound man. Today I'm going
> to show you why. We'll cover the three glues I tested, the joint that beat all of them,
> and how to do it with tools you already own — and stick around to the end, because the
> cheapest option was also the strongest, and almost nobody uses it."

**Example B — calm, reflective channel**

> "The last time I'd tried this, I was half my age. Years later, hands shaking, I tried
> again. Tonight I want to walk you through what I learned: why I'd waited so long, what
> finally got me moving, and how I made myself begin. And do stay with me to the end — the
> last thing I'll share is the one I wish someone had told me decades ago."

Same skeleton, completely different voice.

## Drop-in instruction (for any script-writing agent or prompt)

> "Every script must open with a Retention Opening: (1) optional signature open, (2) a hook
> ending in a promise, (3) a plain preview of the 3–4 chapters in order, (4) an open loop
> that saves the best payoff for last without revealing it. Deliver that saved payoff near
> the end and call back to the hook's promise. Keep the mechanics fixed across all personas;
> adapt only the wording to the character's voice."
